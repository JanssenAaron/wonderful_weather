Cloud Formations

An important way to remain aware about the weather situation in which you may find yourself is cloud formations. Wispier clouds may indicate fair weather, while large intimidating cumulonimbus clouds may indicate an encroaching storm. Being aware of the clouds around you allows you to stay safe even without official warning systems.

Contrails

These clouds are often formed by aircraft, and in fair weather may be some of the only clouds in the sky.

Cirrus

These clouds are very wispy and high in the atmosphere. May indicate a coming change of weather.

Altostratus

Dark and "gloomy" clouds, indicative of precipitation, be it rain or snow.

Cumulonimbus

Cumulonimbus clouds are indicators of a strong storm. Have a plan in place to take shelter if conditions should deteriorate.

Wall Cloud

Small downward offshoots of a larger base cloud. These are associated with strong rotation in a storm, and potentially indicative of a supercell thunderstorm.

Funnel Cloud

Rapidly rotating offshoot. Typically cause for issuance of a tornado warning in the area in which it is spotted. Seek shelter if you see one.

Tornado

Although the larger variants are most well-known, any tornado of any size is potentially dangerous and shelter should be sought immediately if you see one.
